Our Mission

HealthXHer is a multi-disciplinary hackathon powered by the AstraZeneca Campus Ambassadors 2025, dedicated to closing critical gaps in women's health research and treatment.

We believe that solutions to complex challenges in pharmacology, AI, and clinical trial design require diverse expertise. This event brings together students and professionals from engineering, science, business, and design to collaboratively develop real-world, high-impact prototypes.

Our mission is to promote health equity, foster innovation, and drive sustainable change, ensuring better health outcomes for women globally. Join us to transform data into life-changing advancements.

The Challenge Tracks

Select one of four high-impact areas to develop an interdisciplinary solution for female health challenges.

1. Autoimmune Trials: Sex-Sensitive Endpoints and PROs

Objective: Develop endpoints and methods that better capture women‚Äôs disease experiences, where symptoms can vary significantly by sex.

  • Data Science: Analyzing patterns in symptom profiles and disease progression.
  • Biotech/Scientific: Developing sex-sensitive clinical assessments.
  • Policy: Proposing frameworks for mandating sex-sensitive endpoints in clinical trials.

2. Oncology Safety: Early Detection of Sex-Specific Adverse Events

Objective: Redefine the detection and monitoring of sex-specific treatment-related adverse effects in oncology patients.

  • Data Science: Developing algorithms for early warning signals of adverse effects.
  • Biotech/Scientific: Creating frameworks for sex-specific adverse event reporting and causality.
  • Policy: Proposing regulatory updates for sex-specific safety monitoring.

3. Innovating in Cancer Screening and Early Detection for Women

Objective: Enhance screening access and education to reduce inequities in early cancer detection and diagnosis.

  • Data Science: Designing targeted outreach efforts to underserved populations.
  • Biotech/Scientific: Exploring innovative, non-invasive screening technologies.
  • Policy: Developing supportive policies for accessible, community-based screening programs.

4. Improving Autoimmune Disease Diagnosis and Management in Women

Objective: Streamline diagnosis and management pathways, emphasizing timely and personalized care for autoimmune diseases, which predominantly affect women.

  • Data Science: Creating diagnostic support tools leveraging multimodal patient data.
  • Biotech/Scientific: Proposing integrated, patient-centric care models.
  • Policy: Developing clinical guidelines for comprehensive, gender-aware management.

Judging Criteria

1. Innovation

Originality and novelty of the solution compared to existing approaches.

2. Feasibility

Technical viability and practical implementability in a real-world setting.

3. Clinical Relevance

Potential to meaningfully improve patient outcomes or pharmaceutical processes.

4. Technical Execution

Quality of code, prototype, or model demonstrated in the submission.

5. Sustainability

Long-term viability and potential for scalability/monetary impact.

6. Presentation Quality

Clarity, structure, and professional delivery of the final pitch.

FAQ & Official Rules

Essential guidelines for participation, submission, and selection criteria.

Mandatory Team Size

All teams must consist of exactly 4 participants. We require teams to be multidisciplinary to ensure robust solutions for the complex tracks. No individual applications accepted.

Selection Criteria

Teams are accepted on a first-come, first-served basis. Priority is also given to balancing expertise and ensuring an even distribution of teams across the challenge tracks (Approx. 30 total teams).

In-Person Commitment

The Semi-Finals (Lund/Gothenburg/Stockholm) and the Grand Final (AZ Gothenburg) are mandatory physical events. Travel assistance (20 train tickets total) may be provided to finalists.

Project Submission

Participants need to submit the project on the designated platform within the deadline for both the Semi-Finals and the Final. Teams receive a mentor during the final week.
